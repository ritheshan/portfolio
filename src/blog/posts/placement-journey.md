## **What I Learned From His Story** üéØ

This journey taught me things no placement PPT ever will:

- **CGPA matters more than you think.** Many high-package on-campus companies use CGPA as the *first filter* ‚Äî no OA, no chance. Try to keep it **above 8**. Even a *0.xy* difference can decide whether you get to write the test.

- **Take guidance from seniors.** Connect with seniors who are currently facing the market or just got placed. Their grasp of the *current* reality and interview patterns is often more valuable than general advice.

- **Core subjects will come from your semesters.** But **coding** is something *you* must build outside the syllabus.

- **Master one language deeply.** Choose **C++ / Python / Java** ‚Äî just one. Coding skill > Syntax knowledge.

- **Start early.** Begin in **3rd semester**. By the time you reach **5th semester**, you should already be comfortable with DSA and OAs.

- **Participate in hackathons.** They give you real stories to tell in interviews. Projects + experience > certificates.

- **Don‚Äôt wait for ‚Äúperfect preparation.‚Äù** Write every OA. Attend every interview. Even failures teach you the pattern.

- **Luck can make placement easy, but effort makes it inevitable.** Some get it early. Some get it late. Some get it after everything breaks.

But the ones who **stay** ‚Äî they always reach.

You are not late. You are just being prepared for the right moment.
